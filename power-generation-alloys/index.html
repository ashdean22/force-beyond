High-performance alloy components that maximize efficiency and reliability across gas, steam, and nuclear power generation.

Power Generation Demands

The global push for higher thermal efficiency in power generation drives demand for components that operate at ever-increasing temperatures and pressures. Modern combined-cycle gas turbine plants achieve thermal efficiencies above 60% by firing at inlet temperatures exceeding 2,500°F—conditions that demand the most advanced superalloy materials in the hot gas path. Steam turbine components operate at lower temperatures but must endure decades of continuous service under creep conditions. Nuclear plants require materials that maintain integrity under neutron irradiation for plant lifetimes of 40 to 60 years.

Components We Supply

ForceBeyond manufactures power generation components across all three turbine types. Gas turbine components include blades, vanes, combustor liners, transition pieces, and hot gas path structural elements in nickel and cobalt superalloys. Steam turbine components include valve bodies, valve trim, blade groups, and casing forgings. Nuclear plant components include reactor vessel studs, control rod mechanism parts, and steam generator internals. Our manufacturing processes span vacuum investment casting, closed-die and isothermal forging, and precision CNC machining.

Efficiency Through Materials

Higher firing temperatures mean higher efficiency, and higher efficiency means lower fuel costs and lower carbon emissions per megawatt-hour. ForceBeyond's advanced superalloy manufacturing capabilities help power generators push the thermal efficiency frontier by providing the high-temperature components that make next-generation power plants possible.
